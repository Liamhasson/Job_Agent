# Cover Letter Voice Rules
# CRITICAL: The AI must internalise these rules before writing any cover letter.

## What Liam sounds like
- Confident without being arrogant
- Specific: references real numbers, real decisions, real outcomes
- Curious about the company — shows he actually read what they do and why it matters
- Self-aware — can reference an unconventional background (band, freelance) and make it an asset
- Dry wit where appropriate — not trying to be funny, but not robotic either

## Hard rules

1. NEVER open with "I am writing to apply for..." or any variation
2. NEVER open with "I am excited/thrilled/delighted to..."
3. NEVER use the phrase "I would be a great fit"
4. NEVER use "passionate about" unironically
5. NEVER grovel or over-thank — end confident, not desperate
6. NEVER invent experience or skills that don't exist in the CV
7. ALWAYS open with: a human insight, a bold claim, or a personal angle tied directly to the company or role
8. ALWAYS reference something specific about the company that shows genuine research (their product, their stated mission, a recent decision, their design language, their values)
9. ALWAYS stay under 400 words
10. ALWAYS end short and confident — one punchy closing line + "Best, Liam Hasson"
11. ONE PAGE maximum — no padding, no fluff, no filler sentences

## Tone calibration by role type
- Engagement/gaming/consumer apps: lean into the narrative, personal hook, storytelling (GameDuell style)
- Product/AI-native/shipping-focused: punchy, outcome-led, self-aware (Bending Spoons / Avaloq style)
- When in doubt: bold opening claim + 2 relevant project stories + specific company reference + confident close

## What makes a good opening (examples of the pattern, not to be copied)
- "I think about what makes someone want to stay rather than just what gets them to start something new." (insight)
- "Avaloq's mission — democratising access to wealth management — is fundamentally a design problem." (reframe their mission)
- Bending Spoons style: "I use AI — Claude, ChatGPT, Stitch — not as a shortcut but as a core part of how I design..." (bold claim)
